Pacific Northwest


Washington state is known for its nature scenery and its connections with serial killers (I kid you not humans remains were found at a Seattle beach a few days ago). In the PNW, the ocean, forests, mountains, and even deserts are within reach. I live in western Washington, so forests and hiking trails are easy to access. Here are a few (shaky) pictures I've taken of the PNW with short captions:


Poo Poo Point, Issaquah - This was taken at the base of the Poo Poo Point hike. The hike got much more steeper, and going up takes around 1.5 hours.

Vancouver, CA - Over the school year, a few friends and I took a quick road trip to Vancouver and visited the Sea to Sky Gondala attraction (tickets were half off!). I froze my toes off.

Top of Poo Poo Point - This was taken at the top of the Poo Poo Point hike. The black figure is one of the paragliders that jumped off this point.

Snoqualmie Falls, Snoqualmie - Snoqualmie Falls is a popular tourist destination. It's about a 10 minute drive from my home.

Columbia River Scenic Outlook - Eastern Washington's climate is much hotter and dryer. This photo was taken on the drive over there. Pictured is my dad and one of my siblings.

Seattle Skyline - This is a picture of the downtown Seattle skyline. If you zoom in, you can see the Space Needle!

Twin Falls, North Bend - Twin Falls is a relatively short hike with a nice view of waterfalls at the top. The trail mostly follows the river pictured here.

Cherry Blossoms, UW Seattle - My university is known for its cherry blossoms that flower every spring. There's always swarms of tourists, which can make it difficult to get to classes.

Snoqualmie - Pictured is a snowy street in Snoqualmie.
